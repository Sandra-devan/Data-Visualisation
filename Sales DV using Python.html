#Heat Map
#A Heat map is ideal for visulaizing realtionships or patterns in data
pivot_table = df.pivot_table(
    values="Total",
    index= "Gender",
    columns="Product line",
    aggfunc="sum",
)

#Plotting the heat map
plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))
sns.heatmap(pivot_table, annot= True, fmt= ".1f", cmap="PuBuGn", cbar= True)

plt.title("Heat Map: Gender and Product Catergory Vs Total Expenditure", fontsize= 16)
plt.xlabel("Product line", fontsize =12)
plt.ylabel("Gender", fontsize=12)
plt.show()

#Findings:
#The chart indicates that most customers in the supermarket make small purchases, with the majority of sales falling between 0200 units, 
#while high-value purchases are rare. This suggests the supermarket's revenue largely relies on frequent low-value transactions, highlighting 
#potential opportunities to promote higher-value purchases.


#The boxplot shows that Members and Normal customers at the supermarket have similar median spending and comparable variability in spending
# However, Normal customers have more high-spending outliers compared to Members. Additionally, Members 
#exhibit a slightly higher maximum spending amount than Normal customers.


#The pie chart illustrates the proportion of transactions across different product categories in a supermarket. The largest share is in Fashion 
#Accessories (17.8%), followed by Food and Beverages (17.4%) and Electronic Accessories (17%). Sports and Travel (16.6%) and Home and Lifestyle (16%)
#have moderate shares, while Health and Beauty (15.2%) has the smallest proportion. This indicates a fairly balanced distribution among categories.

#In heatmap it is evident that female shops more on food and beverages and men shops more on health and beauty

#Of all the three branches Naypyitaw makes more sales than the other 2 branches

#january has high sales made then there is gradual decrease in sales 

# Based on the chart, Members (shown in purple) generally maintain higher total spending amounts compared to Normal customers (shown in blue). 
#While there are some fluctuations and crossover points, the Member line tends to stay more consistently elevated, especially in the latter months 
#shown on the chart. The shaded areas representing variability also suggest that Members have a more stable spending pattern compared to Normal
